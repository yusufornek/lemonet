# Ethical and legal use

lemonet can monitor and control devices on the network it is connected to. That capability is
useful for legitimate network administration and harmful when misused. This document defines the
boundary.

## Authorized use only

Use lemonet **only** on networks that you own or that you have **explicit, written authorization**
to manage and test. Intent and authorization are what separate legitimate administration from an
attack, and they are what courts and employers will look at.

Legitimate use cases include:

- Managing bandwidth and access on a network you own.
- Diagnosing connectivity and inventorying devices on your own LAN.
- Teaching and demonstrating network concepts in a lab you control.
- Authorized security assessments with written permission from the network owner.

## Prohibited use

- Interfering with, intercepting, or disrupting traffic on networks you do not own or are not
  authorized to manage.
- Unauthorized access, surveillance, harassment, or denial of service against any person.
- Any use that violates applicable law.

## First-run consent

On first launch, lemonet requires you to confirm that you own or are authorized to manage the
network and that you accept these terms before any functionality is available.
